Further Reading and Resources

This appendix provides a curated list of recommended books, academic papers, and online communities to deepen your understanding and engagement with humanoid robotics. This field is vast and rapidly evolving, so continuous learning is key.

1. Recommended Books​

These books cover foundational theories, practical implementations, and advanced topics in robotics and artificial intelligence.

  • "Introduction to Robotics: Mechanics and Control" by John J. Craig
    • A classic textbook covering fundamental concepts of robot kinematics, dynamics, and control. Essential for a solid theoretical base.
  • "Probabilistic Robotics" by Sebastian Thrun, Wolfram Burgard, and Dieter Fox
    • Focuses on the mathematical fundamentals of robotics, especially state estimation, localization, mapping, and planning in uncertain environments.
  • "Modern Robotics: Mechanics, Planning, and Control" by Kevin M. Lynch and Frank C. Park
    • A modern approach to robotics, available for free online, covering foundational material in mechanics, kinematics, dynamics, and control, with a strong emphasis on computational aspects.
  • "Reinforcement Learning: An Introduction" by Richard S. Sutton and Andrew G. Barto
    • The seminal textbook on reinforcement learning, providing a comprehensive introduction to the theory and algorithms.
  • "Deep Learning" by Ian Goodfellow, Yoshua Bengio, and Aaron Courville
    • A comprehensive textbook on deep learning, covering a wide range of topics from mathematical foundations to advanced research concepts.

2. Key Academic Papers and Journals​

Staying updated with recent research is crucial. Here are some key journals and conferences where cutting-edge humanoid robotics research is published.

Journals:​

  • IEEE Transactions on Robotics (T-RO): One of the most prestigious journals in robotics, covering all aspects from theory to application.
  • International Journal of Robotics Research (IJRR): Another top-tier journal for fundamental robotics research.
  • Robotics and Autonomous Systems (RAS): Focuses on intelligent robotics, mechatronics, and automation.

Conferences:​

  • International Conference on Robotics and Automation (ICRA): IEEE RAS's largest conference, covering a broad range of robotics topics.
  • International Conference on Intelligent Robots and Systems (IROS): Another major IEEE/RSJ international conference on robotics.
  • Robotics: Science and Systems (RSS): A highly selective conference emphasizing the scientific and mathematical foundations of robotics.
  • Conference on Robot Learning (CoRL): Focuses specifically on machine learning for robotics.

3. Online Communities and Resources​

Engaging with the community can provide support, new ideas, and networking opportunities.

  • ROS Wiki and ROS Answers:
    • ROS Wiki: The official documentation hub for ROS, with tutorials, packages, and concepts.
    • ROS Answers: A Q&A platform for ROS users to ask questions and get help.
  • Robotics Stack Exchange:
    • Robotics Stack Exchange: A community-driven Q&A site for robotics professionals and enthusiasts.
  • GitHub:
    • Explore repositories from leading robotics labs and companies. Many open-source robotics projects are hosted here.
  • arXiv:
    • arXiv Robotics: A preprint server where researchers share their latest papers before peer review. Excellent for tracking emerging trends.
  • YouTube Channels:
    • Many universities and researchers maintain YouTube channels demonstrating their latest humanoid robot projects and tutorials.

By leveraging these resources, you can continue your journey into the fascinating world of humanoid robotics beyond the scope of this book.
